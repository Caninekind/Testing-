Is Private Training Right for You?

Private lessons are an excellent option for dogs who struggle with:

Not listening or ignoring commands

Jumping on guests or family

Pulling on leash during walks

Not coming when called

Difficulty settling in the house

Potty training challenges

Chasing small animals

Other behaviors you want to address

Your Commitment Matters

Success in private lessons starts with you!

Just like personal fitness training, dog training requires consistency and commitment. Attending a session once a week isn't enough—real progress comes from daily practice and reinforcement at home.

Between each lesson, your trainer will provide a detailed training plan. Regular practice with your dog is essential for success during the program.

What Can We Help With?

Foundational Obedience

Master essential commands:

  • Sit
  • Down
  • Come
  • Off
  • Drop it
  • Leave it

Relationship Building

Build a strong bond with your dog by learning how to set and maintain boundaries, communicate effectively, and play together.

Impulse Control

Teach your dog an "on and off switch" to help regulate their hyperactivity and settle calmly in the house.

Play & Motivation

Play is critical for building a bond. We'll help find what motivates your dog—whether it's food, toys, or personal play.

E-Collar Training

Learn the effective and humane way to use an e-collar to unlock a world of freedom for your dog. When used knowledgeably, it's a highly effective communication tool.

Body Language

Understand what your dog is saying through facial expressions, posture, tail position/wag, and movements to better read their emotions.
